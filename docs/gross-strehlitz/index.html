District Capital in Upper Silesia • Micro Artistic Actions

About Gross Strehlitz

Gross Strehlitz (Polish: Strzelce Opolskie, German: Groß Strehlitz) is a town in Upper Silesia and the capital of Strzelce County in the Opole Voivodeship of Poland. Located approximately 30 kilometers southeast of Opole (Oppeln), it serves as an important administrative and cultural center for the surrounding region.

Historical Significance

First mentioned in historical records in the 13th century, Gross Strehlitz developed as an important market town and later industrial center. The town's German heritage is evident in its architecture and urban planning, while its current Polish identity shapes its contemporary cultural life. The district includes numerous smaller towns and villages, including Leschnitz (Leśnica), each with their own unique histories.

Modern Strzelce Opolskie

Today's Strzelce Opolskie is a vibrant district capital that balances its historical heritage with modern development. The town hosts various cultural events, maintains active civic organizations, and serves as an economic hub for the region. The micro actions from this area reflect the dynamic interplay between tradition and progress.

District Towns and Villages

  • Zawadzkie (Andreashütte) - Industrial heritage town

  • Kolonowskie (Colonnowska) - Former iron production center
  • Jemielnica (Himmelwitz) - Cistercian monastery town

Regional Connections
